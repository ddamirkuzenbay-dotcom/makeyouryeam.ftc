How to Create an FTC Team

This guide is designed for school teachers and mentors who want to bring robotics, engineering and real STEM impact into their community through FIRST Tech Challenge.

Main Part

STEP 01

Gather Members

Start by finding motivated students interested in STEM. An FTC team usually includes 5â€“15 students with different strengths: engineering, programming, design, and outreach.

  • Announce the opportunity at school
  • Invite students from different grades
  • Focus on curiosity, not experience
STEP 02

Talk With the Principal About Sponsorship

School administration support is essential. Explain what FTC is, how it benefits students, and what the school gains.

  • Present FTC as an educational program
  • Discuss space, time, and basic funding
  • Highlight STEM visibility for the school
STEP 03

Assign Team Roles

Clear roles help the team stay organized and productive. Students can rotate roles during the season.

  • Builders / Mechanical Team
  • Programmers
  • Design & CAD
  • Outreach & Documentation

Working Process

STEP 04

Watch Our Team Implementation Guide

Learn how to officially implement an FTC team using FIRST standards. This video is designed for teachers and mentors starting from zero.

STEP 05

Build the Robot & Program It

Design, assemble and test your robot. Program autonomous and driver control modes, iterate constantly, and encourage students to experiment and learn from failures.

STEP 06

Compete, Learn & Win

Participate in FTC competitions, share your impact with judges, connect with other teams, and inspire your local community through STEM.
